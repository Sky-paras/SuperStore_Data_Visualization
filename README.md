# 📊 Superstore Sales & Profit Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing sales
and profit performance across different dimensions such as time,
customers, product categories, and geographic regions. The goal is to
uncover key business insights and support data-driven decision-making.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🎯 Objectives

-   Analyze sales trends over time
-   Identify profitable and loss-making customers
-   Evaluate product performance by sub-category
-   Understand regional profit distribution
-   Enable dynamic filtering for deeper insights

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📊 Dashboard Components

### 1. Sales Trend (Time Series Analysis)

-   Sales show an overall upward trend from 2014 to 2019.
-   Significant spikes observed in 2018 (\~118K).
-   Seasonal fluctuations indicate demand variability.

### 2. Sales vs Profit by Customers (Scatter Plot)

-   Positive correlation between sales and profit for most customers.
-   Several customers generate **high sales but negative profit** →
    loss-making segment.
-   Helps identify high-risk customers.

### 3. Profit by Sub-Category (Heatmap)

-   Categories like **Copiers and Phones** are highly profitable.
-   Sub-categories such as **Tables and Bookcases** show consistent
    losses.
-   Regional differences highlight performance variation.

### 4. Profit by States (Map)

-   Western region shows strong profitability.
-   Some states (e.g., Texas) show negative profits.
-   Geographic analysis helps in regional strategy planning.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🎛 Filters Used

-   Category
-   Segment
-   Month of Order Date

These filters allow dynamic slicing of the data for deeper analysis.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 💡 Key Business Insights

-   High sales do not always mean high profit → cost management
    required.
-   Certain product categories consistently underperform and need
    review.
-   Regional profitability varies significantly → location-based
    strategies needed.
-   Customer segmentation can help identify profitable vs risky
    customers.
